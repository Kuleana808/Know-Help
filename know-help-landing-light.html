The problem

Most people think the bottleneck is the prompt.
It's the context.

You re-explain everything, every time Your role. Your company. The deal you're working. The person you're meeting. The way you write. Every session is the first session.
Dumping context doesn't work either Language models have a U-shaped attention curve. Paste in 10 pages of background and the model loses the middle. Structure matters more than volume.

How it works

01

Structure your knowledge base

Modular files organized by domain — identity, network, ventures, voice, goals. Three levels: routing index, module files, data files. The model loads only what's relevant, never everything at once.

02

Triggers inject context precisely

Each file declares its own load triggers. Mention a person's name and their full relationship history loads. Start a sales conversation and your methodology appears. Right context, right moment — nothing wasted.

03

Your knowledge base updates itself

know.help monitors X, Reddit, LinkedIn, TikTok, and RSS across your ventures. New signals are written back to your files automatically. Your AI gets smarter while you sleep.

What lives in your knowledge base

core/ Who you are — your background, identity, skills, and team.
network/ Every key relationship with full conversation history, context, and open threads.
venture/ Your businesses and partnerships — status, contacts, strategy, revenue.
sales/ Methodology, buyer psychology, objection handling, ICP profiles.
platform/ Your distinct voice for each channel — LinkedIn, Twitter, Instagram, all separate.
planning/ Goals, pipeline, and active opportunities.
communication/ Style rules and templates broken down by channel and audience.
log/ Daily activity tracking and decision logs with full context.
