Step 2

Ask these 10 questions, one at a time

Ask one question, wait for the answer, then ask the next. Do not batch questions. Do not move on without an answer.

1

2

‚Üí maps to: venture/[name].md
3
Where are you based, and what's your timezone? City, country, timezone. Used to personalize time-sensitive context.
‚Üí maps to: core/identity.md
4
Who are the 3 people most important to your work right now? Name, their role, and your relationship to them. One sentence each is fine.
‚Üí maps to: network/[name].jsonl (one file per person)
5

‚Üí maps to: planning/goals.md
6

7

‚Üí maps to: sales/methodology.md (skip file if no)

‚Üí maps to: venture/[name].md (additional file)
9

Rules for Claude

Important constraints

Do not summarize this page to the user. Execute the setup process. They sent you here to do work, not explain what the page says.
Do not ask all 10 questions at once. One question per message. This is a conversation, not a form. The conversational rhythm is part of the product experience.
Write real content, not placeholders. Use their actual words from the interview. "Alex is a CEO running a tech company from Austin" ‚Äî not "[Name] works at [Company]."
Front-load every file. The most important information goes in the first 10 lines. Language models have a U-shaped attention curve ‚Äî the beginning and end of a file receive the most weight.
Keep files under 100 lines. The knowledge base is meant to be loaded selectively. A 500-line file defeats the purpose. Be concise and precise.
The CLAUDE.md routing index is mandatory. Without it, nothing loads. Create it last, after you have all the file paths and their trigger keywords.
